Sortition

I advocate for sortition: forming legislative bodies by lot. Sorition bodies would be short-lived, compensated, and optional.

This would supplant the use of elections to form legislative bodies. I argue that not only are weaknesses in voting methods salient in elections, but using elections themselves results in unrepresentative legislative bodies. By definition, elected legislatures are made up of those who had the time and means to successfully run for office. Furthermore, these people share a psychological preference of wanting to run for office. This results in a class of legislators who favor the wealthy and power-seeking. This is endemic to elections and at odds with the goal of democracy.

Sortition is based on the properties of mathematical sampling. If you pick roughly a thousand elements of a population, you can represent them with great accuracy.

Yes, this is a radical idea and there's a lot of issues that jump out. What if stupid people get selected? What if psychopaths get selected? What if you get selected?

Georgism & The Land Value Tax

I used to joke that I felt like I was living on a Monopoly board. Then one day I read an article explaining the origins of Monopoly and the ideas of Henry George. It seems so obvious now: we live on Earth, none of us made Earth, we all require the Earth, yet we've malformed government to allow some people, and not others, ownership of the Earth and all she has to offer.

The original version of Monopoly was the Landlord's Game created by Lizzie Magie in 1904. It contained a second phase. In the second phase, Henry George's Land Value Tax was implemented. I think we need to implement this tax now.
